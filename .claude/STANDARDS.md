## Case Study Standards

### Every case study MUST have:
- [ ] Opening with tension — the problem stated as a real cost/pain, not a project description
- [ ] Clear personal scope — "I decided X" not "we explored Y"
- [ ] The key decision — what was chosen over what alternative, and why
- [ ] Real constraints — time, tech, org, resources. No constraints = school project energy
- [ ] Outcome with numbers — even rough, even qualified ("~40% in usability testing, n=6")
- [ ] Technical credibility — at least one sentence about implementation, architecture, or technical tradeoff
- [ ] 5-minute maximum read time

### Case study red flags (auto-fail):
- Opens with "In this project I..."
- No constraints mentioned
- "Improved user experience" with no specificity
- "We did X" without personal contribution clarity
- Process-heavy with no shipped outcome shown
- Metrics that sound fabricated or suspiciously round

## Copy Standards

### Banned phrases:
- "Passionate about..."
- "User-centered design" (as differentiator)
- "Human-first..."
- "I help companies..."
- Any sentence that could apply to any UX designer

### Standards by copy type:

Hero text: 5-10 words, specific, memorable. NOT "Designing experiences that matter"
Bio: 60-90 words, differentiator first. NOT resume recitation.
Project tagline: Problem + outcome in 1 sentence. NOT "A redesign of X"
Outcome statement: Specific + honest. NOT "Improved satisfaction"
CTA: Tells exactly what happens next. NOT just "Contact Me"

## Design Presentation Standards

Every case study visual presentation must:
- [ ] Show the actual shipped product, not just wireframes
- [ ] Include component/system thinking (not just pretty screens)
- [ ] Show at least 2 states: ideally empty/loading/error/success
- [ ] Be honest about fidelity — low-fi is fine if labeled as such

## Honesty Protocol

This portfolio is built on real work. No fabrication. No inflation.

On metrics:
- Use real numbers from real measurement
- If measurement was imperfect: "In usability testing (n=6), we observed..."
- If no metrics exist: "We didn't have instrumentation — what we could measure was X"
- Never round up. Never omit sample size. Never claim causality without evidence.

On contribution:
- Be precise about personal vs team contribution
- "I designed X" / "The team built Y, I owned Z" — clarity protects credibility

On outcomes:
- Show what actually shipped, not the ideal version
- If the project was cancelled or descoped: say so. It shows real-world experience.
